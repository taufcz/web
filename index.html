Understanding Freemasonry

The Craft of Freemasonry

Freemasonry is one of the world's oldest fraternal organizations, tracing its formal origins to the early 18th century. It is a system of moral teachings illustrated by symbols, primarily derived from ancient stonemason guilds.

Central to Freemasonry is the concept of building oneself into a better person through self-knowledge, moral discipline, and brotherhood. The organization is non-political and non-religious, welcoming men of all faiths who believe in a Supreme Being.

Our meetings combine ceremonial rituals that convey moral lessons, discussion of Masonic philosophy, planning of charitable activities, and friendly social interaction among brothers.

Becoming a Member

Who Can Join?

Freemasonry welcomes men of good character who believe in a Supreme Being. There are no restrictions based on religion, political views, race, or social status. We seek members who:

  • Are at least 21 years of age
  • Possess good moral character
  • Have a belief in a Supreme Being
  • Desire to improve themselves and society
  • Join of their own free will and accord

The Path to Membership

1. Initial Contact

Reach out to us through our contact form. A member of our Lodge will respond to answer your questions.

2. Meet the Brothers

You'll be invited to informal gatherings to meet members and learn more about our Lodge.

3. Petition

If you wish to proceed, you'll submit a formal petition, which must be recommended by two existing members.

4. Investigation

A committee will meet with you to discuss your interest and answer any questions you may have.

5. Initiation

If accepted, you'll undergo the first degree ceremony to become an Entered Apprentice.
